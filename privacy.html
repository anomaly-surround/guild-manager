Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 8, 2026

1. Information We Collect

When you sign in with Discord or Google, we receive your username, avatar, and email address from the provider. We do not request or store your password.

We also store data you create within the app: teams, members, boss timers, events, chat messages, and uploaded files.

2. How We Use Your Information

  • To provide and operate the Guild Manager service.
  • To identify you within your teams.
  • To process payments through Paddle (our Merchant of Record).
  • To send notifications you've configured (e.g., Discord webhooks).

3. Data Storage

Your data is stored on Cloudflare's infrastructure (D1 database and R2 storage). Data is stored securely and access is restricted to authenticated users within your teams.

4. Third-Party Services

  • Discord & Google — for authentication. Their privacy policies apply to data they collect.
  • Paddle — for payment processing. Paddle acts as Merchant of Record and handles all payment data. See Paddle's Privacy Policy.
  • Cloudflare — for hosting and data storage.

5. Data Sharing

We do not sell, rent, or share your personal information with third parties for marketing purposes. Data is only shared with the third-party services listed above as necessary to operate the service.

6. Data Retention

Your data is retained for as long as your account is active. You can delete your data by leaving teams or contacting us. If you delete your account, your data will be removed.

7. Cookies

We use local storage (not cookies) to keep you signed in. No tracking cookies or analytics are used.

8. Your Rights

You have the right to access, correct, or delete your personal data at any time. Contact us to make a request.

9. Children's Privacy

Guild Manager is not intended for children under 13. We do not knowingly collect data from children under 13.

10. Changes

We may update this policy. Continued use after changes means you accept the updated policy.

11. Contact

For privacy-related questions, reach out via the Discord community or GitHub.
